### **Executive Summary (2020)**

The 2020 calendar year at the Maxson and Stiles Wastewater Treatment Facilities was marked by the operational challenges of the **COVID-19 pandemic**, including the declaration of a Civil Emergency in March and the implementation of split-shift staffing protocols[cite: 5, 6]. Infrastructure at Maxson faced severe strain, highlighted by the **primary sludge wasting line breaking multiple times** and the "cage drop" in Primary Clarifier #1[cite: 8, 9, 15]. Key milestones included the commissioning of the **new 23 kV electrical sub-station** and the transition to **gravity effluent discharge**, which ended nearly two years of continuous pump monitoring[cite: 13, 18]. Despite these improvements, the facility struggled with high loading, recording **130 permit excursions** primarily due to process upsets and mechanical failures[cite: 143].

---

### **Equipment & Infrastructure**

- **Clarifiers:** Structural failure occurred in Primary Clarifier #1 when all four suspension arms sheared off, dropping the sweep structure to the floor[cite: 28, 29]. Repairs were also made to the scum hopper and sweeps for Secondary Clarifier #1[cite: 42].
- **Pumps & Motors:** Significant rebuilds were completed for Raw Sewage Pump #1, ABF Pump #8, and the #1 Screw Pump lower flight assembly[cite: 23, 10, 15].
- **Blowers:** Control upgrades for all six aeration blowers were completed by Tristate[cite: 21, 412]. Lubrication systems for South Blowers #2 and #3 were rehabbed to address chronic leaks[cite: 414].
- **Electrical:** A new **23 kV switchgear** system and main power metering were put into service in August[cite: 13, 381].
- **Disinfection:** The disinfection tank and effluent discharge improvements were completed, allowing a switch to gravity flow[cite: 18, 386].

---

### **Contractors & Stakeholders**

- **US Peroxide (USP):** Initiated a pilot project involving hydrogen peroxide injection to reduce sulfur emissions at Lagoon #5[cite: 5, 206].
- **CDM Smith:** Completed construction of the disinfection tank and began work on Coarse Bar Screen installation (Package 2)[cite: 163, 167].
- **AECOM:** Conducted a kick-off meeting to evaluate influent sampling methodologies for permit compliance[cite: 11, 379].
- **Tri State:** Finished the electrical upgrades for aeration blower controls[cite: 21, 412].
- **PXC:** Conducted full-scale Phase 1 disinfection performance testing in November[cite: 165, 166].

---

### **Failure Modes & Operational Challenges**

- **Industrial Slug Discharges:** Frequent events throughout January, February, and May caused detergent foam in aeration basins and heavy grease accumulations that interfered with bar screens[cite: 2, 4, 7, 10].
- **Primary Sludge Line Failures:** The aging 15-inch wasting line suffered its ninth break in August, necessitating continuous temporary repairs while waiting for a full replacement design[cite: 9, 15, 383].
- **COVID-19 Impact:** Staffing was split into alternating 7-day shifts to prevent a total facility outbreak, and a Civil Emergency was declared to access federal funding[cite: 5, 6].
- **Plant Debris:** Duckweed from the former Earth Complex drainage area fouled secondary clarifiers from August through October, requiring removal via vactor truck[cite: 16, 17, 384].

---

### **Key Metrics & Performance**

- **Flow:** The average daily flow rate held steady at **72 MGD**, with a base flow of 55 MGD[cite: 72, 73].
- **Loading:** Average influent BOD loading rose to **528,381 lbs/day** (a 5.5% increase), while TSS loading decreased slightly to **409,184 lbs/day**[cite: 77, 83].
- **Removal Efficiency:** The facility maintained a removal efficiency of **94% for BOD** and **92% for TSS**[cite: 78, 84].
- **Energy Consumption:** Monthly power consumption averaged **6.13 million kWh**, with an average monthly bill of **$410,385**[cite: 474, 476].
- **Maintenance:** Total work orders peaked at **9,671**, with over 7,400 focused on preventative maintenance[cite: 439, 440].

---

### **Phenomena Tracked**

- **Biogas Yield Declines:** Biogas production dropped to **438 million cubic feet**, reaching levels not seen since 2003. This was attributed to a decrease in biogas produced per pound of sludge destroyed[cite: 514, 524, 130].
- **Struvite Formation:** Continued monitoring of struvite in high-turbulence areas of Lagoons 3 and 4; plans were made to resume ferrous chloride usage to mitigate this[cite: 68, 69, 70].
* **COVID-19 Monitoring:** The facility participated in a national initiative to track COVID-19 concentrations in raw wastewater to correlate with human case counts.
- **Lagoon Solids Accumulation:** Net accumulation of dry solids in the lagoons averaged **26.6 million dry pounds per year**, indicating a need to double dewatering rates to reach stability[cite: 123, 125].
