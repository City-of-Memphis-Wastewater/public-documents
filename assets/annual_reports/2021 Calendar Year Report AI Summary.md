### **Executive Summary (2021)**

The 2021 calendar year was defined by extreme weather events and significant infrastructure challenges, most notably a **record cold snap in February** that dropped temperatures to near zero, causing system-wide process upsets and the declaration of a mayoral emergency[cite: 9, 10, 11]. Operationally, the year was a period of high regulatory pressure, ending with a surge in **permit excursions**—reaching 134 in December alone—primarily due to the failure of the trickling filter system[cite: 41, 46]. 

Key milestones included the commissioning of the **PAA Disinfection system** and the finalization of effluent flow modeling to satisfy NPDES accuracy requirements[cite: 23, 60, 481]. Despite these hurdles, the city completed over 6,000 maintenance work orders and successfully recommissioned the Biorem system[cite: 33, 494].

---

### **Equipment & Infrastructure**

-**Pumps & Motors:** Maintenance performed extensive work on raw sewage pumps (RSP #1–3), screw pumps #1–7 (including gear box and motor base reconfigurations), and return sludge pumps[cite: 437, 441, 451, 459, 492].
-**Blowers:** The facility struggled with blower capacity, limited to operating only four at a time due to air flow restrictions from new fine bubble diffusers and transformer winding failures[cite: 19, 50, 52].
- **Disinfection System:** A new PAA (Peracetic Acid) disinfection tank and dosing system were commissioned in August 2021[cite: 481].
- **Treatment Units:** Media demolition was initiated for Bio-towers #5 and #6, and the Biorem odor control system completed its performance trial in December[cite: 26, 40, 55].
- **Electrical:** A new **23KV Electrical Sub-Station** was commissioned in October, serving as the main feeder for the entire plant[cite: 482].

---

### **Contractors & Stakeholders**

- **Scott Contractors:** Issued a Notice to Proceed (NTP) to replace the primary sludge wasting line, with completion in September 2021[cite: 20].
- **CDM Smith:** Performed wet well cleaning and commissioned new coarse bar screens[cite: 437].
- **Roots:** Conducted inspections of the aeration system to diagnose blower and transformer issues[cite: 51, 52].
- **Biorem:** Collaborated on the recommissioning and performance testing of the biogas treatment system[cite: 33, 55].
- **Industrial Users:** Significant loading impacts were noted from **Memphis Bioenergy** (slug discharges), **Kellogg** (rice dryer fire), and **Solae**[cite: 6, 12, 30].

---

### **Failure Modes & Operational Challenges**

- **Weather Extremes:** Record snow and freezing temperatures in February led to reduced water pressure, causing multiple blower failures and process upsets[cite: 9, 11, 196].
- **Industrial Slug Discharges:** Frequent "unusual" influent characteristics, including grease with "BBQ" or "French fry" odors, caused drops in dissolved oxygen and effluent discoloration[cite: 5, 29, 207].
- **Structural Failures:** The floor of the primary clarifiers began breaking up, releasing rocks and aggregate that damaged bio-pump impellers[cite: 44, 415].
- **Regulatory Non-Compliance:** The facility recorded **412 total permit excursions** in 2021, with a dramatic spike in the fourth quarter following the total failure of the trickling filter process[cite: 189, 413, 420].

---

### **Key Metrics & Performance**

- **Flow:** Average daily flow was **70 MGD**, a slight decrease from the previous year[cite: 121].
- **Removal Efficiency:** BOD removal averaged **91%**, while TSS removal was **89%**[cite: 127, 133].
- **Loading:** Average influent BOD loading was **592,967 lbs/day** (a 12% increase), and TSS loading was **534,703 lbs/day** (a 31% increase)[cite: 126, 132].
- **Energy & Utilities:** Main power consumption averaged **5.6 million kWh per month**, costing approximately **$427,146 monthly**[cite: 529, 531].
- **Maintenance:** A total of **6,247 work orders** were completed, of which 5,067 were preventative[cite: 494].

---

### **Phenomena Tracked**

- **Biogas Production:** The facility produced **430 million cubic feet** of biogas in 2021, continuing a long-term downward trend since 2015[cite: 567, 569, 181].
- **Struvite Formation:** Monitored primarily in Lagoons 3 and 4, favoring high-turbulence areas[cite: 118, 498].
-**Effluent Modeling:** Intensive tracking of weir discharge coefficients was performed to validate effluent flow meter accuracy[cite: 60, 434].
